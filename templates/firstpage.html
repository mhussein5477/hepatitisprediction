What is Hepatitis ?

Hepatitis is an infectious disease that is caused by viral infection usually in a contaminated water or even food, the virus is deadly as it affects the Liver causing inflammation , which then leads to scarring fibrosis thus killing the liver cells . It may even cause death.

WHO states that, in 2015, hepatitis is responsible for the greatest challenge of disease causing approximately 1.34 million deaths globally. The existing method for detecting and reporting a case takes quite a while, especially for the results to classify positive or negative patient.

What are the symptoms

Your doctor draws a small amount of blood from a vein in your arm and sends it to a laboratory for testing. The results of a blood test can confirm the type of viral hepatitis, the severity of the infection, whether an infection is active or dormant, and whether a person is currently contagious.

  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of Appetite
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Abdominal Pain
  • Dark Urine
  • Light-colored Stools
  • Joint Pain
  • Jaundice
